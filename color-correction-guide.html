What is Color Correction?

Color correction is the process of fixing hair that didn't turn out as intended. This could mean:

  • Removing unwanted brassy or orange tones
  • Fixing patchy or uneven color
  • Reversing damage from box dye
  • Going back to natural after years of coloring
  • Fixing hair that's too dark, too light, or the wrong tone

How to Choose a Color Correction Specialist

  1. Look for corrective color experience: Not all colorists do corrections. It's a specialty.
  2. Ask about their process: A good colorist will explain what needs to happen and set realistic expectations.
  3. Check before/after photos: Specifically look for corrections similar to your situation.
  4. Expect a consultation first: Any reputable colorist will want to see your hair before quoting.
  5. Be prepared for multiple sessions: Some corrections can't be done in one day.

DIY Color Correction: Why You Shouldn't

We strongly advise against attempting color correction at home. Here's why:

  • You could cause more damage and make the problem worse
  • Home color removers are harsh and unpredictable
  • You don't have professional-grade products or knowledge
  • Fixing a DIY correction fix costs even more
