What is Balayage?

Balayage (pronounced bah-lee-ahj) is a French hair coloring technique that creates natural-looking, sun-kissed highlights. Unlike traditional foil highlights, balayage is hand-painted onto the hair, creating a softer, more gradual transition from darker roots to lighter ends.

The word "balayage" comes from the French word meaning "to sweep" or "to paint," which perfectly describes the technique. A skilled colorist literally sweeps color onto the hair freehand, allowing for customized placement that flatters your face shape and skin tone.

Balayage Aftercare Tips

  • Wait 48-72 hours before washing your hair after balayage
  • Use sulfate-free, color-safe shampoo
  • Apply a purple shampoo 1-2x per week to prevent brassiness
  • Use heat protectant before styling tools
  • Schedule a toner refresh every 6-8 weeks
  • Deep condition weekly to maintain hair health

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does balayage last?

Balayage typically lasts 3-4 months before you need a touch-up. Because the color is painted away from the roots, grow-out is much more graceful than traditional highlights.

Can balayage damage my hair?

Any lightening process can cause damage, but balayage is generally gentler than all-over bleach. A skilled colorist will assess your hair's condition and use bond-building treatments (like Olaplex) to minimize damage.

Is balayage good for dark hair?

Yes. Balayage on dark hair creates beautiful dimension with caramel, honey, or chocolate tones. It may take multiple sessions to achieve very light ends without damage.
