How to Communicate What You Want (Without Sounding Crazy)

This is the most important section. Miscommunication is the #1 cause of salon disappointment. Here's how to fix that:

‚úì EFFECTIVE DESCRIPTIONS:

  • ‚Ä¢ "I want to be able to air-dry and have it look good"
  • ‚Ä¢ "I want low maintenance‚ÄîI only style once a week"
  • ‚Ä¢ "I want face-framing brightness but keep my base dark"
  • ‚Ä¢ "I want noticeable change but not unrecognizable"
  • ‚Ä¢ "I want to look polished but not high-maintenance"

‚úó VAGUE DESCRIPTIONS:

  • ‚Ä¢ "Just a trim" (what's a trim to you?)
  • ‚Ä¢ "Make it lighter" (how much lighter?)
  • ‚Ä¢ "Nothing too crazy" (subjective!)
  • ‚Ä¢ "Whatever you think" (we need more input!)
  • ‚Ä¢ "The usual" (on a first visit?)

The Magic Questions to Ask

"How much will this cost, including everything?"

Don't be shy about this. Ask for a price estimate including cut, color, toner, treatment, etc. Surprises at checkout are awkward for everyone.

"How long will this take?"

Important for scheduling and expectations. Color services can take 3-4 hours; don't book one if you need to leave in 90 minutes.

"What will the grow-out look like?"

Especially for color. If you can't commit to touch-ups every 6 weeks, certain techniques won't work for you.

"What maintenance will this require?"

Special shampoo? Weekly treatments? Daily blow-drying? Know what you're signing up for.

"Is this achievable today, or will I need multiple sessions?"

Realistic expectations prevent disappointment. Some transformations can't happen in one visit without destroying your hair.

The Consultation Should Feel Collaborative

A good stylist will ask YOU questions, explain options, discuss pros/cons, and make sure you're on the same page BEFORE starting. If someone just nods and starts cutting without discussion, that's a red flag.

What to Expect During Your Appointment

The Typical Flow

1

Check-In & Wait (5-10 min)

You'll be offered water/coffee and given a form if it's your first visit. Don't worry if your stylist is running a few minutes behind‚Äîthat means they don't rush clients.

2

This is where you show photos, discuss what you want, talk about your lifestyle and maintenance commitment. Your stylist will touch your hair, assess texture, and explain their plan.

3

Relax and enjoy. This is your moment to zone out. If the water temperature is uncomfortable, speak up.

4

The Service (30 min - 4 hours depending)

Cuts: 30-60 minutes. Color: 2-4 hours. You'll have processing time where you can read, work on your phone, or chat with your stylist.

During the service, it's okay to:

  • ‚Ä¢ Ask to see the back in a mirror
  • ‚Ä¢ Request adjustments ("Can we go a little shorter?")
  • ‚Ä¢ Take breaks if you need to use restroom
  • ‚Ä¢ Ask questions about what they're doing
5

Styling & Finishing (15-30 min)

Your stylist will blow-dry and style. This is when you should ask them to show you how they're achieving the look so you can replicate it at home.

6

Product Recommendations & Checkout (5-10 min)

They'll recommend products. You're not obligated to buy, but if they're suggesting something specific to your service, it's worth considering.

What If Something Goes Wrong?

It happens. Maybe the cut is shorter than you wanted. Maybe the color didn't turn out as expected. Here's how to handle it:

Step 1: Speak Up (Immediately if Possible)

If you notice something during your appointment‚Äî"This is shorter than I wanted" or "This color looks more orange than golden"‚Äîsay so right away. It's easier to fix in real-time than after you've left.

Step 2: Give It 24-48 Hours (For Color)

Hair color can look different once you wash and style it yourself. Most salons ask that you live with it for a day or two before requesting corrections. This is reasonable.

Step 3: Call and Explain Calmly

Most reputable salons will offer to fix issues for free within 1-2 weeks of your service. Be specific about what's wrong:

  • ‚Ä¢ "The color is more brassy than the golden tone we discussed"
  • ‚Ä¢ "The layers are shorter than the photo I showed you"
  • ‚Ä¢ "I'm experiencing significant breakage since my treatment"

Step 4: Document Everything

Take photos of the issue. Keep your receipts. If the salon refuses to help or makes it worse, you have documentation for reviews or potential disputes.

What Good Salons Do:

  • ‚Ä¢ Apologize and schedule a fix appointment immediately
  • ‚Ä¢ Don't charge for corrections within reason
  • ‚Ä¢ Take responsibility rather than blaming you
  • ‚Ä¢ Work with you to make it right
