Understanding Review Volume

The Math on Reviews:

A salon with 1,500 reviews seeing 30 clients/day would accumulate that many reviews in about 2-3 years if 10-15% of clients leave reviews. A boutique salon seeing 5-8 clients/day with the same review rate takes much longer to build numbers - but each review represents more personal attention.

High review count means high volume. It doesn't necessarily mean better quality - it means more people went through the door. The question is: do you want to be one of 30+ clients that day, or one of 5-8?

Large/Chain-Style Salons: Pros & Cons

✓ Advantages

  • • Often easier to get appointments
  • • Usually open more days/hours
  • • May have more service options
  • • Social proof from many reviews
  • • Sometimes lower prices (volume model)

✗ Disadvantages

  • • May see different stylist each time
  • • Assembly-line feel, less personal
  • • Junior stylists often do the work
  • • Less time per client
  • • Quality can be inconsistent

Boutique/Owner-Operated Salons: Pros & Cons

✓ Advantages

  • • Same expert every visit
  • • Owner has reputation on the line
  • • More time and attention per client
  • • Personalized recommendations
  • • Often higher skill level (owner is the talent)
  • • Better for complex work (corrections, transformations)

✗ Disadvantages

  • • Harder to get appointments (limited availability)
  • • May have fewer hours/days
  • • If you don't click with the owner, limited options
  • • Sometimes slightly higher prices

When to Choose Each Type

Choose a Large Salon If:

  • ✦ You need an appointment today/this week
  • ✦ You're getting a basic service (simple cut, root touch-up)
  • ✦ Price is your primary concern
  • ✦ You don't mind seeing different stylists
  • ✦ You want a bustling, social atmosphere

Choose a Boutique Salon If:

  • ✦ You want the same expert every time
  • ✦ You're getting color work (balayage, corrections)
  • ✦ You want undivided attention
  • ✦ You value a relationship with your stylist
  • ✦ You've been burned by inconsistent results before
  • ✦ You have difficult or damaged hair

The Quality Question

Here's something to consider: at a large salon, who actually does your hair?

Many high-volume salons use a "levels" system:

  • Level 1 (Junior): Recent graduates, still learning
  • Level 2 (Stylist): A few years experience
  • Level 3 (Senior): Experienced, higher prices
  • Master/Director: Top level, premium prices, often booked months out

When you book at a large salon, you might get a Level 1 or 2 stylist unless you specifically request (and pay for) their top talent. At an owner-operated boutique, you get the master by default.
